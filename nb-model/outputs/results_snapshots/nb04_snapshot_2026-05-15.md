## Preliminary Answer to RQ4

The CV score does **not** significantly improve model fit on the pilot data (LR p = 0.30, ΔAIC = −0.92). However, this result is **uninformative** due to:

1. **n = 22** — far too small to detect a moderate effect
2. **Near-zero score variance** (σ ≈ 0.017) — the predictor has almost no usable variation; virtually all of it is measurement noise relative to the CV model's MAE (0.189)
3. **Severe attenuation bias** — classical errors-in-variables pushes the coefficient toward zero; the true effect is likely larger than estimated
4. **NB2 non-convergence** — Hessian is singular at this sample size

The direction of the CV score effect is consistent with the hypothesis (safer-looking → fewer crashes). The full survey will widen both n and the score range, which is where RQ4 can be properly tested.
